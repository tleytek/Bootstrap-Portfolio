    Who am I?

    I am a Full Stack Developer whose proficient in the M.E.R.N. tech stack, and always eager to learn more.

    Why Software Development?

    I initially pursued a career in Electrical Engineering and graduated from Durham College with a diploma. I chose this path because I found myself fascinated with the Arduino Uno, a healthy mixture of software development and electronics. The course advertised itself as such but took a turn for the worst as the later half of the program taught mechanical engineering. Dissapointed that my program did not provide me with the skills I needed, I worked as a delivery driver for a few years. During this time I was always thinking what I wanted to do for a career. I had to look within and ask myself everyday "What do I like to do?". In my spare time I would enjoy tinkering with other peoples software for 3D printers, keyboard designs in Clojure, and Scripts for automating tasks. I felt as though I enjoyed working with software more than anything, but I had to make sure it wasn't just a phase. So I kept working as a delivery driver until I could confirm with myself that software development is what I wanted to do. Lo and behold, I chose to pursue Software Development.

    Why Web Development?

    My reasons for pursuing Web Development are quite vapid, but that doesn't mean I don't enjoy every moment learning about it. I told myself that I wanted to make software that could help the largest amount of people, within my capabilities. Out of all the options for education, UTM's Full Stack Web Developer Bootcamp was perfect for it's schedule and location.
