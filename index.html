Hello, and welcome to my website! My name is Mateus Koehler, I am currently working as a truck driver while attending a Coding Bootcamp at UTM (University of Toronto Mississauga) by Trilogy Education Services. I have a college diploma in Electronics Engineering, so I really like tinkering and opening electronics to see whats inside. In highschool, my progamming classes had been my most enjoyable, and looking back I can't figure out why I didn't pursue further education in it. So here I am now, taking the coding bootcamp to change careers!

When I got to looking at coding as a career, I couldn't help but feel frustrated at the lack of mainstream adoption in keyboard innovation. It's a programmers primary tool isn't it? I found the default QWERTY keyboards to be inefficient and counter-intuitive to our language. Keyboard layouts are horizontally staggered which was a design choice made due to way type-writers functioned. Our keyboards don't have the limitations type-writers had, so why are we still using it? There are many improvements that I see could be done to the keyboard, but the main take away is that form should follow function, not the other way around.
